The Plan

As stated in Week 3, my website serves as a place for people to read about my experience learning about web development, and follow my progress. With this website, I will be building a portfolio for myself, outlining my web development capabilities, my essay writing and my design. I want the website to feel personal, like a conversation. I hope that people will read my blogs and feel like they learn about the internet and about me at the same time.

Goals for My User

My goal is to make a website that can be easily navigated. I don’t want the user to feel overwhelmed by the visuals, the context or the navigation of the site. This is a place for users to see projects I have been a part of, and see my thought process.

My User

I believe there is no way to truly define my user, because you can never control who comes across your website. However I can define my target audience. My target audience is split into 2 groups; people who benefit from my site, people that my site benefits from. People that benefit from my site are those who want to see my journey of interactive media, and want to learn from my choices. They will mostly be interacting with my weekly blogs as well as my Design section. The people who my site benefits from are companies, influencers and future employers. They will be using my site to see my workflow, my projects, my writing and design capabilities. All of these opportunities could lead to collaboration, employment and reaching a larger audience.

My Content

    My content is made up of 5 categories:
  • Blog
  • Design
  • Essay
  • Portfolio
  • About Me

The Blog and Design sections are documentation of my journey learning web development, and they outline my thought process. The Blog section will include some references to academic writing here and there. The Essay sections will be essays I write for my Interactive Media course. It will be a way for people to see my essay writing skills, as well as a chance for me to explore and dive into academic writing. The Portfolio section is to showcase and highlight my skills and achievements. This will include different sites where you can see my work (GitHub, Behance.net and itch.io). The About Me section will contain my resume, my social media links, my interests and of course, a bio about me!

Interface Elements and Information Structure

Input Controls:

In my style guide, there is a section for interface. This shows you what my buttons look like and my in line hyperlinks. I plan to use hover and cursor changes in my css to make it clear that you are hovering over a clickable item. The interactive elements will be clearly highlighted through use of colours, font styles and shapes.

Navigational Components

I have a navigation bar to get you from one page to the next, using href links in my html. I also want to utilise breadcrumbs, by underlining the active page.

Informational Components

I will be using project cards on the Blog homepage so that the user sees an image, a quick narrative hook, a title and the date published. I do want to have a loader so that while the user is waiting for the site to load, there's something nice for them to see.
