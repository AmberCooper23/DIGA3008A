Welcome to Week 13 of Interactive Media! Let's discuss what an ethical internet looks like to me.

For me, ethical internet is open and accessible to everyone, no matter their circumstances. It ensures that people with different abilities, backgrounds, or financial situations can all use and access the internet fairly.

Accessibility is about designing with different people in mind. For example, a blind person might use a screen reader, so websites should include alt text for images. An ethical internet makes sure to use these features.

It also means the internet should be accessible to everyone, regardless of where they are. This ensures that nobody is left behind on important information.

An ethical internet uses simple and clear language so that everyone can understand its content, regardless of their reading level. In conclusion, ethical internet is for everyone. It’s meant to be inclusive and accessible to everyone.
