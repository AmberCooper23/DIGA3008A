Welcome to Week 7 of Interactive Media! For this section, I will be diving into design.

The Plan

As stated in Week 3's blog, my website serves as a place for people to read about my experience learning about web development, and follow my progress. With this website, I will be building a portfolio for myself, outlining my web development capabilities, my essay writing and my design. I want the website to feel personal, like a conversation. I hope that people will read my blogs and feel like they learn about the internet and about me at the same time. I wanted to communicate through round shapes and fun tertiary colours that I am a bubbly and fun person. I feel like my use of rounded shapes also show that I'm young (and therefore still learning).

Goals for My User

My goal is to make a website that can be easily navigated. I don’t want the user to feel overwhelmed by the visuals, the context or the navigation of the site. I plan to use a constant nav bar that the user can utilise on every page. I also make use of page headings, which remind the user of where they are on my side. This is a place for users to see projects I have been a part of, and see my thought process.

My User

I believe there is no way to truly define my user, because you can never control who comes across your website. However I can define my target audience. My target audience is split into 2 groups; people who benefit from my site, people that my site benefits from. People that benefit from my site are those who want to see my journey of interactive media, and want to learn from my choices. They will mostly be interacting with my weekly blogs as well as my Design section. The people who my site benefits from are companies, influencers and future employers. They will be using my site to see my workflow, my projects, my writing and design capabilities. All of these opportunities could lead to collaboration, employment and reaching a larger audience.

My Content

    My content is made up of 5 categories:
  • Blog
  • Design
  • Essay
  • Portfolio

The Blog and Design sections are documentation of my journey learning web development, and they outline my thought process. The Blog section will include some references to academic writing here and there. The Essay sections will be essays I write for my Interactive Media course. It will be a way for people to see my essay writing skills, as well as a chance for me to explore and dive into academic writing. The Portfolio section is to showcase and highlight my skills and achievements. This features my art portfolios, my writing, my games, my game assets and maybe some websites I have worked on in my own time. The Portfolio section will also contain cv, a way to contact me, my interests and of course, a quick bio about me!

Interface Elements and Information Structure

Input Controls:

In my style guide, there is a section for interface. This shows you what my buttons look like and my in line hyperlinks. I plan to use hover and cursor changes in my css to make it clear that you are hovering over a clickable item. The interactive elements will be clearly highlighted through use of colours, font styles and shapes. I wanted my hyperlinks to stand out, so I made their text purple. As for my nav bar, I used hover to temporarily show the button UI, and used active to indicate what page the user is on. Details like this are important, as they communicate with the user and encourage interaction.

I chose a font that was rounded, whilst still remaining legible and neat. I believe that communicating playfulness in my site is a great way to hook users. When it comes to font sizes, I was very careful not to use anything that was too small or too big. There is a spectrum that goes from illegible to in your face, and I wanted to maintain a perfect middle.

Navigational Components

I have a navigation bar to get you from one page to the next, using href links in my html. I also want to utilise breadcrumbs, by highlighting the active page.

Informational Components

I will be using project cards on the Blog homepage and the Design homepage so that the user sees an image, a title and the date published. Because my target audience is people interested in web development, I believe the best way to hook them is through consistent visuals. I chose one theme and stuck to it. I do want to have a loader so that while the user is waiting for the site to load, there's something nice for them to see. I also ensured that there is alt text for every image, to ensure that users with screen readers are able to use my website in full functionality. I tried my best to ensure that my colour contrasting was good, making sure there was no light on light or dark on dark overlays.
