My Style Guide

Below is a breakdown of my style guide. I made it using the Canvas app. I tried to capture the colour palette, the typography, the iconography and the imagery. I want the images on my site to be minimalist yet artsy. I want to try to stick to the sketched look in my pictures, as I feel it represents my artsy side.

Designed by Amber Cooper 17 th March 2025

Let's outline my interface elements and information structure

Input Controls:

In my style guide, there is a section for interface. This shows you what my buttons look like and my in line hyperlinks. I plan to use hover and cursor changes in my css to make it clear that you are hovering over a clickable item. The interactive elements will be clearly highlighted through use of colours, font styles and shapes.

Navigational Components:

I have a navigation bar to get you from one page to the next, using href links in my html. I also want to utilise breadcrumbs, by underlining the active page.

Informational Components:

I will be using project cards on the Blog homepage so that the user sees an image, a quick narrative hook, a title and the date published. I do want to have a loader so that while the user is waiting for the site to load, theres something nice for them to see.

My goal for my headings is to keep it simple but organised. I used block background for Heading 2 as those are gonna feature in text heavy sections, and will break up the text really well.

The font I chose is Atkinson Hyperlegible. It's easy to read, minimalist and works well in capital letters, bolded or italics. This font can also be interpreted by screen readers which is important for accessibility and digital ethics.

The colours for light mode are purples and blues, to communicate a gentle and calm environment. I want the website to feel casual and easy to digest, so I stayed away from harsh colours.
